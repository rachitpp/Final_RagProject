# Foreign Daily Allowance (DA) — country category joined with band rates (HRP/017)

This is a single joined view of the foreign DA matrix: each row pairs a country
category with the countries in it AND the per-day DA for every band. Use it so
the country category and its dollar rate are read from the SAME row. (Faithful to
foreign.pdf p.1 rate table + p.2 country-category table.)

**To find the DA:** first decide the country's category from the "Countries in
this category" column, then read the dollar amount in the column for the
employee's band — both from that one row.

| Country category | Countries in this category | Band 9 & 10 | Band 8 | Upto Band 7 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| A | Europe, Taiwan, Japan | $250 | $200 | $125 |
| B | U.S.A., Canada, Singapore, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, UAE, Qatar, Nigeria | $200 | $150 | $100 |
| C | All other countries not classified in A or B (e.g. Brazil, Australia, Thailand, South Africa) | $175 | $125 | $75 |

**Excluded:** Nepal & Bhutan are explicitly NOT covered by this foreign DA matrix
(they are excluded from Category C) — there is no foreign DA entitlement for them.

**Eligibility (§Eligibility):** this policy applies to employees in Band 7, 8, 9
& 10. For any other band, foreign travel is approved through a separate Foreign
Travel sanction (the standard DA matrix above does not automatically apply).

**DA reductions when the company/host provides facilities (§1.3):**
- Both boarding AND lodging provided -> **30%** of the DA above (§1.3a).
- Lodging only provided -> **50%** of the DA above (§1.3b).
- Boarding only provided -> **75%** of the DA above (§1.3c).

Worked example: a Band 9 employee visiting Brazil (Category C -> $175) with both
boarding and lodging provided gets 30% x $175 = **$52.50** per day. A Band 7
employee in the same case gets 30% x $75 = **$22.50** per day.
